Section 0
Headline Findings — November 2025

🔍 Critical Insights — Act on These

November was the highest-volume and highest-impression month of the entire dataset. 24 LinkedIn posts generated 64,302 organic impressions and 1,226 total engagements (per competitor data) — both the highest of any month tracked. This was driven by a combination of major announcements, event content, and consistent document posting.
Three major announcements landed in November: Deloitte Fast 50 #27 (Nov 7), HubSpot partnership (Nov 11), and the first US event in Texas (Nov 10 recap). Each drove above-average impressions. The Deloitte post drove strong reposts; the Texas event recap hit 27.6% CTR.
The Nov 20 "safety net" document post achieved the highest CTR of November at 33.5% and 34.8% ER — a thought leadership carousel about career safety nets. This confirms the pattern: relatable career-challenge content consistently outperforms announcement content on click-through rate.
⚠️ Despite the highest volume and impressions, avg ER dropped to 14.5% — the lowest of any month in the dataset. More posts dilute the per-post ER average. The 3 video posts averaged only 3.8% CTR, dragging the overall metric down. November had 21 documents which averaged 14.1% CTR.
Instagram had 10,478 total views across 7 posts (4.48% avg ER) — consistent with other months. The Nov 5 carousel (6.27% ER) and Nov 7 Deloitte image (4.90% ER, 2,206 views — highest IG views of the month) were the standouts. Press/milestone images continue to generate high IG reach.
