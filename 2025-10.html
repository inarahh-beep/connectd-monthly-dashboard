Headline Findings — October 2025

🔍 Critical Insights — Act on These

October holds the all-time records for both organic impressions (71,421) and new LinkedIn followers (2,147) across the entire dataset. The peak of 501 new followers on Oct 15 alone — driven by the Festival of Entrepreneurs event — shows what live events do to growth. Events are a primary follower acquisition channel.
Despite leading in impressions, October's avg ER of 10.5% was the second lowest on record. High volume of posts (23) across high impression days spreads engagement thinner. The data confirms: more posts = more impressions but lower per-post ER average.
The Oct 21 "5 female CEOs" carousel hit 35.4% CTR — the top performer of the month. Audience-relatable career challenge content consistently outperforms company news on click-through. This is the same pattern as the Nov 20 "safety net" post (33.5%) and Jan 22 Founder Social recap (44.2%).
October's Instagram had 5 saves and 11 shares — above average across the dataset. The 7 posts averaged 3.13% ER (reach-based), consistent with the lower-ER months. No viral outlier, but steady consistent performance.
In the competitor benchmarks, Connectd led on new followers (2,147) by a significant margin among comparable-size accounts. Only Jack & Jill (2,870) and Wellfound (1,714) came close — and both have different follower bases. The event-driven growth strategy is working for follower acquisition.
