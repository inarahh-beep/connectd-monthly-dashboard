Headline Findings — January 2026

🔍 Critical Insights — Act on These

The Founder Social recap post (Jan 22) hit 44.2% CTR and 46.4% ER — the highest ER of any LinkedIn post across the entire Q1. Event recap content with genuine community energy massively outperforms planned content. The same day's William Smith profile post hit 35.6% CTR — two 35%+ posts in a single day is exceptional.
Times Tech 100 (#18) announcement (Jan 16) drove 7,576 impressions — highest single-post reach of the quarter — and 88 likes, 13 comments, 26 reposts. But only 2.7% CTR. This confirms the same pattern seen with FT 1000 and Sifted 100: press recognition is widely shared but rarely clicked through.
⚠️ January had the lowest impression total of all five months (43,083) — but the highest click-to-impression efficiency on document posts (21.8% avg CTR). This suggests January content was extremely targeted and relevant, even if volume was lower. The fractional work misconception series (Jan 26–28) all hit 30%+ CTR.
Two new partnerships announced in January: SeedLegals (Jan 15, video) and NachoNacho (Jan 21, video). Both were video posts with 3–5% CTR — they got good engagement (like/repost ratio) but didn't drive clicks. Partnership announcements are better suited to video format for community warmth, but shouldn't be expected to drive link traffic.
Jack & Jill gained 5,343 new followers in January — a 15.7% growth rate — against Connectd's 1,230 (2.7%). This is unusually high and likely driven by a viral post or external campaign. Worth monitoring to understand what drove it.
