Headline Findings — July 2025

🔍 Critical Insights — Act on These

August was the quietest month for follower growth — +715 new followers, the lowest of the entire 11-month dataset. This is a clear seasonal pattern: August is a summer slowdown month across B2B social. All other months averaged 1,200+ new followers. Factor this into monthly targets.
Despite lower volume, the Aug 13 document post hit 46.9% CTR and 47.9% ER — "Pay is where most people get fractional leadership wrong." This is the second-highest CTR in the dataset (behind Sep 1's 56.1%). Direct challenge to a common misconception continues to be the highest-performing LinkedIn format.
August had 7 video posts out of 15 total — the most video-heavy month of the dataset (47% video). Yet doc posts (8) averaged 16.8% CTR vs video's much lower average. The summer content mix leaned into video but the data consistently shows documents drive more clicks.
Instagram had 7,489 views across 10 posts — one of the higher-view months despite low shares (3). The Aug 1 reel (5.82% ER, 4 saves) and Aug 22 reel (4.15% ER) were the standouts. August IG performance was more consistent than other months — no viral outlier but steady 3–4% ER across most posts.
Intro dominated on engagements again in August — 1,124 total engagements across 17 posts (66.1 eng/post), nearly 3× Connectd's 27.8. This gap is consistent across Q3 2025. Understanding what Intro posts differently could unlock significant LinkedIn engagement improvement.
