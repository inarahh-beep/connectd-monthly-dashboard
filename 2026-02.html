Headline Findings — February 2026

🔍 Critical Insights — Act on These

February had the highest LinkedIn ER of any month this quarter — 19.4% avg across 20 posts. Five posts exceeded 30% CTR and four exceeded 40%. The advisor spotlight format (Kristin M Berkinsky / Beth J. Brown / Evelyn Kim, Feb 2) hit 41.4% CTR and 42.8% ER — the highest combined performance of Q1.
The Times Square billboard post (Feb 27) drove 7,282 impressions — the second-highest single-post reach of the quarter — and 129 likes, 20 reposts. But only 7.1% CTR, confirming brand announcements get shared but rarely clicked. Same pattern seen with FT 1000 and Sifted 100 in March.
The 40,000 LinkedIn followers milestone was announced Feb 23 — the video post got 77 likes and 9 reposts (strong) but only 2.9% CTR. Milestone posts generate engagement but don't drive clicks. Peak new-follower day: Feb 10 (162 new followers) — driven by the NYC event promo, not the milestone announcement itself.
February had only 4 Instagram posts — the lowest volume of the quarter. Avg ER was 3.13%, also the lowest quarter month. All 4 posts were Genesis Health / fractional explanation content. The month lacked the variety (carousels, press moments) that drove higher March IG engagement.
The NYC and Miami event push worked: the Feb 25 event day-of post hit 30.6% CTR; the Feb 10 event promo hit 37.8% CTR. Event content with a clear CTA (register link) consistently outperforms educational content on click-through. This pattern is consistent across all four months.
